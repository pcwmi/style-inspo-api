# Style Inspo: Product Vision

*This document captures the evolving product vision as we build from 0 to 1. Updates track how our understanding of the problem and solution develops over time.*

## Current Vision (v1.0) - Personal Styling Assistant

**Target User**: Initially building for myself - someone who loves fashion but struggles with daily styling decisions and wants to maximize their existing wardrobe.

**Core Philosophy**: "Whatever outfit we generate still needs to feel like me" - All styling must be grounded in understanding the user's innate style and comfort zone.

## Three Core Use Cases

### 1. "How Item" Styling
**Problem**: "I have items I really like but don't wear enough because they feel 'too much' or 'too dressy'"
- Example items: cowboy boots, special dresses, statement pieces
- Current behavior: These items sit unworn because they feel hard to style casually
- Desired outcome: Get daily inspiration for incorporating beloved-but-underused pieces

**User Story**: "Hey, I'm waking up and I want to wear my cowboy boots today. How do I style them so they feel like me and not like a costume?"

### 2. Mood-Based Styling
**Problem**: "I want to dress for how I want to feel today, connected to my personal style"
- Daily emotional goals: relaxed, fun, tender, confident, professional, creative
- Constraints: Weather, personal style DNA, available wardrobe
- Current behavior: Default to safe choices that don't match desired mood

**User Story**: "Today I want to feel confident and a bit playful. It's 72Â°F and sunny. What can I wear from my closet that honors my soft, elegant style but pushes me toward that playful energy?"

### 3. Inspiration Translation
**Problem**: "I see fashion inspiration (Instagram, runway, street style) but don't want to buy everything new"
- Inspiration sources: Phoebe Philo collections, Instagram influencers, Pinterest mood boards
- Desired outcome: Create "homage" looks using existing wardrobe
- Value: Creativity and sustainability over consumption

**User Story**: "I'm inspired by this Phoebe Philo collection aesthetic. What can I create from my closet that captures this mood without buying anything new?"

## Underlying Technical Requirements

**Style DNA Understanding**:
- Map user's comfort zone (regular wear items)
- Identify aspirational direction (style growth goals)
- Track daily emotional preferences and patterns

**Styling Intelligence**:
- Balance authenticity with growth ("feels like me, but elevated")
- Understand garment relationships and proportions
- Consider practical factors (weather, occasion, lifestyle)

## Future Evolution Potential

**Near-term (3-6 months)**:
- Expand beyond personal use to friends/family with similar style sensibilities
- Add more sophisticated mood and occasion detection

**Long-term (6-12 months)**:
- Broaden target user base while maintaining personalization depth
- Add social/sharing features for inspiration exchange
- Integrate with broader fashion/shopping ecosystem

## Success Metrics (Personal Use)

**Engagement**:
- Daily app usage for outfit decisions
- Increased wearing frequency of "how items"
- Feeling excited about getting dressed

**Styling Quality**:
- Outfits feel authentically "me" while pushing comfort zone
- Successful mood-based dressing
- Creative satisfaction from inspiration translation

**Long-term Impact**:
- More confident daily style choices
- Better utilization of existing wardrobe
- Reduced impulse fashion purchases
